Physical Security

Physical security are measures used to deny unauthorised access to hardware, software, networks, and data physcial actions that can result in data corruption or damage to a business.

How physical security secures our information systems

General overview with sections below going into more detail

Why is Physical Security Important?

Physical security is often overlooked, by establishing this it prevents serious data loss or damage to an enterprise, it also protects various assets and sensitive inforamtion about clients, employees, and even higher ups. It ensures protection from vandalism, theft or natural disasters.

How to Implement Physical Security

By providing access control, it prevent or reduces the chances of outsiders of gaining sensitive material or information. You can add surveillance cameras, in case of any theft, you can have footage and reveal the perpetrator. Adding water sprinklers or fire exit can help against fires, saving data from being lost or assets being damaged.

4 Layers of Physical Security

Deterrence; placing keys in a safe place or implementing key control systems reduces chances of criminals gaining access to high security rooms. Access control; the monitoring of who accesses what and restrict unwanted access. Detection; triggering sound alarm when an intruder gain unauthorised access. Identification; using security camera or biometrics to identify criminals.
